Our history

The idea for Daily Driver came out of an everyday watercooler conversation between the founders. They were discussing their ideas for various applications and hardware devices but none of them really caught the other's attention. Finally, Dan mentioned his idea for a device that could interpret the check engine light for you so there was no need to take your car to a service station or purchase expensive equipment to read the code. This idea did catch their attention and as they talked about the potential and technical challenges they all started to realize that a company was being born.

Armed with only a great idea and a couple months of concept generation, the BlueRoot team decided to apply to the Barris Incubator at Dartmouth. After a solid presentation by Adam and a meet and greet with several of the mentors and staff of the incubator, the BlueRoot team was selected as a winner and brought into the 2012-13 class. The incubator has been a tremdous opportunity for the BlueRoot team and the resources being brought into the company will help to craft a quality product that will make it easier for everyone to understand their vehicle.
