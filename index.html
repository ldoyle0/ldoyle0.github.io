Project Overview

Develop a novel water filter than can work purely on gravity with a maximum of 3ft of head.

Project Details

There was a hole in the water filtration market for a cheap filter that could work purely on flow supplied by gravity for individuals. Existing products on the market generally require a pump to force the water through the filter with an acceptable flow rate. The reason a pump is needed is because of the cylindrical shape of a typical filter cartridge. For this product, a flat configuration was chosen which increased the surface area, and allowed it to generate a high flow rate with minimal head. The other innovation was a clamping and sealing method that allowed the filter to be assembled with only two plastic parts.



Project Overview

Development of a first of its kind 40kW isothermal compressed air energy storage system (ICAES).

Project Details

I was the lead design engineer for the press and pneumatic cylinder development. The pneumatic cylinders were a highly customized component, and I was tasked with designing them from the ground up. The press that tied the pneumatic and hydraulic cylinders together was initially designed by an outside vendor but the results of that exercise were unsatisfactory so I was tasked with leading up the redesign effort. I modeled and analized the design with CAD and FEA in conjunction with an outside engineering team who access to more powerful FEA tools. We managed to complete the redesign in only 4 months. I also played a role in designing hydraluic manifolding, pipeing layouts, water pumps, and water spray systems.
